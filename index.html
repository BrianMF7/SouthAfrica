PreHistory

Before South Africa became its own country, it was an land at the southernmost region of Africa and the first modern humans have inhabited this area more than 100,000 years ago.South Africa's prehistory experienced and through two phases named the Stone Age and Iron Age.

Union of South Africa

After the Anglo–Boer wars in which a Boer ultimatum that the British should cease building up their forces in the region, Britain decided to unify the four colonies including the former Boer republics into a single self-governed country called the Union of South Africa. As years passed by in 1934 ,The Union of South Africa parliament passed the Status of the Union Act. This act declared South Africa to be "a sovereign independent state". Britain were affected with this act and removed the last vestiges of British legal authority over South Africa. This was the beginning of South Africa's journey to its new nation.

Apartheid era(1948-1994)

The aparthied era in South African history refers to the time that the National Party led the country's white minority government, from 1948 to 1994. The great depression and World War II brought increased economic growth in South Africa, and the government convinced the policies of racial segregation, this is reason of aparthied Between 1960 to 1983, Hendrik Verwoerd started aparthied in South Africa, 3.5 million black South Africans were removed from their homes and forced into segregated neighborhoods as a result of apartheid legislation. Black South Africans could not own property. The apartheid come to an end in early 1990s and that led to the formation of a democratic government in 1994. The end of apartheid in south africa is 1990. The apartheid system in South Africa was ended through a series of negotiations between 1990 and 1993 and through unilateral steps by the de Klerk government.

2010 Fifa World Cup

South Africa was chosen by FIFA president Sepp Blatter at a media conference on 15 May 2004 in Zürich to host the major soccer tournament which was the 2010 Fifa World Cup in which they beat Egypt, Morocco, and a joint bid from Libya and Tunisia. The Fifa 2010 World Cup qualification draw occurred in Durban on 25 November 2007. Since South Africa was the host nation, they qualified automatically for the tournament. About 32 countries qualified for the 2010 Fifa World Cup. To prepare for this wonderful event, Five new stadiums were built and there was some upgrades for five old stadiums. ABout US$1 billion was spent on renovation and the stadium constructions. The hype was real. The 32 teams played and the finals of this tournament took place on July 11 2010 at the stadium called Soccer City, in Johannesburg. Spain defeated the Netherlands 1–0 and secured their first Fifa World Cup tournament.
